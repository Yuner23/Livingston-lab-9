Activities

The town offers a range of outdoor activities, including swimming, snorkeling, and kayaking, as well as hiking and visiting nearby waterfalls. Visitors can also take boat tours to explore the nearby mangrove forests and see local wildlife, such as manatees and dolphins. Livingston is also a great place to sample the local cuisine, which features a mix of Caribbean and Guatemalan flavors, and to experience the vibrant Garifuna culture through music and dance performances. Overall, Livingston offers a unique and relaxing destination with plenty of opportunities to explore the natural beauty and cultural richness of the region.Beware of the strong beverage Grifiti.

Boat Tours

Boat tours are a popular activity in Livingston, Guatemala, due to its location on the Caribbean coast and the nearby Rio Dulce. The tours typically offer a unique opportunity to explore the area's natural beauty and cultural heritage. Visitors can take a leisurely trip through the Rio Dulce canyon or head out to the open sea to visit nearby islands, such as Cayo Quemado or Playa Blanca. Many boat tours also include stops at local fishing villages and provide opportunities to sample traditional cuisine. The tours can be tailored to fit different interests and timeframes, making them a flexible and enjoyable option for visitors to Livingston.

Playa Blanca

Playa Blanca is a beautiful white sand beach located in Livingston, Guatemala. It's known for its crystal clear water and is a popular spot for swimming and sunbathing. The beach is surrounded by lush tropical vegetation and offers stunning views of the Caribbean Sea. Playa Blanca is accessible only by boat, which adds to its secluded and serene atmosphere. Visitors can also enjoy a variety of water activities such as snorkeling, kayaking, and fishing, or simply relax in one of the beachfront hammocks and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

About Livingston

Livingston is a small town located on the Caribbean coast of Guatemala, known for its unique blend of Afro-Caribbean and Mayan cultures. The town is accessible only by boat and is surrounded by lush tropical rainforest, pristine beaches, and the scenic Rio Dulce. Livingston is home to a vibrant community of Garifuna people, who have a distinct language, cuisine, music, and dance. Visitors can explore the town's colorful streets, sample local seafood dishes, relax on the beaches, take a boat tour of the Rio Dulce, or go on a hike in the nearby rainforest. Livingston is also a popular destination for those looking to learn about Garifuna culture and history.
